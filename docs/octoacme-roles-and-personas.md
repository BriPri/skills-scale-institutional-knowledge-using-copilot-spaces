# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises. Each role includes a summary, key responsibilities, goals, typical communication patterns, and interactions with other roles.

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
The Project Sponsor provides executive-level support, resources, and strategic direction for the project. They champion the project within the organization and make high-level decisions when escalation is required.

### Responsibilities
- Provide overall project direction and strategic alignment
- Secure and allocate resources (budget, personnel, tools)
- Champion the project at the executive level
- Review and approve key milestones and deliverables
- Remove organizational blockers and escalate issues when needed
- Make final decisions on scope changes that impact budget or timeline

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers strategic business value
- Maintain executive visibility and support
- Facilitate cross-organizational alignment

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or milestone-based briefings from Project Manager
- Escalation meetings as needed
- Executive steering committee updates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Receives status updates, provides direction, and approves major decisions
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on business outcomes and success metrics
- **Change Manager**: Coordinates on organizational change impact
- **Stakeholders**: Champions project value and secures buy-in

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
The Business Analyst bridges communication between stakeholders and technical teams. They gather requirements, document user needs, and help translate business problems into actionable specifications.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, analyze, and document business requirements
- Translate stakeholder needs into user stories and acceptance criteria
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Identify process improvements and optimization opportunities
- Validate solutions against documented requirements
- Maintain requirements traceability

### Goals
- Ensure requirements accurately reflect stakeholder needs
- Reduce ambiguity in specifications
- Support smooth handoff between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements review sessions with stakeholders
- Collaboration with Product Manager on backlog refinement
- Specification handoffs to Developers
- Validation sessions with QA Lead

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on requirements prioritization and backlog refinement
- **Developers**: Provides detailed specifications and clarifies requirements
- **QA Lead**: Ensures acceptance criteria are testable and complete
- **Stakeholders**: Gathers requirements and validates solutions
- **Change Manager**: Identifies process changes impacting users

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead owns the quality assurance process, coordinates testing activities, and ensures deliverables meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the QA strategy and test plans
- Coordinate testing activities (unit, integration, E2E, UAT)
- Review acceptance criteria for testability
- Track and report on defects and quality metrics
- Ensure deliverables meet the Definition of Done
- Approve releases from a quality perspective

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, defect-free releases
- Establish efficient and repeatable testing processes
- Reduce defect escape rate to production

### Typical Communication
- QA status updates in sprint reviews
- Defect triage meetings with Developers
- Release readiness sign-off with Project Manager
- Test plan reviews with Business Analyst

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on test coverage and defect resolution
- **Business Analyst**: Validates acceptance criteria are testable
- **Project Manager**: Reports on quality status and release readiness
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on acceptance criteria and user expectations

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
The Change Manager oversees change control procedures, manages the impact of project changes on stakeholders, and ensures smooth transitions for process or scope changes.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain change management processes
- Evaluate change requests and assess impact on scope, timeline, and resources
- Communicate impending changes to stakeholders
- Facilitate stakeholder alignment on change decisions
- Monitor adoption and address resistance to change
- Document and track change requests and approvals

### Goals
- Minimize disruption from project changes
- Ensure stakeholder awareness and readiness for changes
- Maintain change control discipline

### Typical Communication
- Change advisory board meetings
- Stakeholder impact assessments
- Change announcements and training coordination
- Post-change reviews

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on change impact and timeline adjustments
- **Project Sponsor**: Escalates significant changes for approval
- **Business Analyst**: Identifies process changes affecting requirements
- **Communications Coordinator**: Aligns on change communication plans
- **Stakeholders**: Communicates changes and gathers feedback

---

## Communications Coordinator

### Role Summary
The Communications Coordinator manages internal and external project communications, ensuring timely status updates, announcements, and feedback loops across the team and stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and execute the project communication plan
- Prepare and distribute status updates, newsletters, and announcements
- Coordinate stakeholder briefings and town halls
- Manage feedback channels and escalate concerns
- Ensure consistent messaging across all communications
- Maintain communication templates and archives

### Goals
- Keep all stakeholders informed and engaged
- Ensure timely and accurate project communications
- Foster transparency and trust

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status reports and newsletters
- Milestone announcements
- Stakeholder feedback summaries
- Internal team communications

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Receives status information and coordinates messaging
- **Change Manager**: Aligns on change communication plans
- **Product Manager**: Coordinates product announcements
- **Stakeholders**: Distributes information and gathers feedback

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Receives requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Project Manager**: Reports progress and blockers
- **QA Lead**: Collaborates on testing and defect resolution
- **Business Analyst**: Clarifies specifications and requirements

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Sponsor**: Aligns on strategic goals and business outcomes
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on delivery and timeline
- **Developers**: Provides requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Business Analyst**: Collaborates on requirements and backlog refinement
- **QA Lead**: Validates acceptance criteria meet quality standards
- **Communications Coordinator**: Coordinates product announcements

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Sponsor**: Provides status updates and escalates major decisions
- **Product Manager**: Coordinates on delivery timeline and scope
- **Developers**: Tracks progress and removes blockers
- **QA Lead**: Coordinates on release readiness and quality status
- **Change Manager**: Aligns on change impact and timeline adjustments
- **Communications Coordinator**: Provides status information for communications
- **Business Analyst**: Ensures requirements are on track
